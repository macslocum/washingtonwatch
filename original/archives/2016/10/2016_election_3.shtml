2016 Election

70% of Americans believe that this election has brought out the worst in people. [Monmouth University]

48% of eligible voters in 2016 are unmarried, up from 30% in 1974. And for the first time the percentage of unmarried women who are eligible to vote exceeds that of married women 26% to 25.9%. The percentage of married men who are eligible continues to exceed the number of unmarried men 26.3% to 21.6%. [WSJ, 10/1-2]

75% of Americans disapprove of the way in which Congress is handling its job. [Fox News, 9/16]


The states of Delaware, Mississippi and Vermont have never sent a woman to Congress.


48% of registered voters prefer a Congress that is controlled by the Democrats while 45% would like to see the Republicans in charge. [NBC/WSJ, 9/16]
